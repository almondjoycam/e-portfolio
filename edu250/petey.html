EDU 250

Special Education Law Application

Acronym What each letter stands for Description of the law How would Petey’s life have been different if this law was in effect when he was young?
(Explain why using the specific language in the law and specific examples from the Petey book)
IDEA Part C Individuals with Disabilities Education Act This section of IDEA details child find and intervention for young children from birth to age 6. Soon after Petey was born, his parents would receive a referral for special education services. The child find program outlined in part C coordinates with many early childhood agencies to ensure that all infants and toddlers are identified. This law also covers infants and toddlers who are wards of the state, like Petey became, in section 303.302(b)(1).
IDEA Part B Individuals with Disabilities Education Act This section of IDEA outlines programs for children with disabilities from ages 3-22. After being evaluated and identified for a disability, Petey's parents and a multidisciplinary team of professionals would convene to draft an individualized education plan (IEP) so that Petey could receive an education.
FAPE
& Dear Colleague Letter		 Free & Appropriate Public Education An education specifically tailored to a student with a disability must be provided, free of charge. Instead of spending his childhood in the monotony of the Warm Springs Insane Asylum, Petey would be provided with an education that includes special education, related services, and supplementary aids and services, at no cost to his parents. They would not have been forced to sell their property and relinquish custody of Petey to the state.
LRE Least Restrictive Environment As much as possible, students with disabilities should be educated with their nondisabled peers. Petey would learn alongside nondisabled peers as much as possible, and may have learned to communicate before Calvin joined him in the men's ward when he was 11.
ADA Americans with Disabilities Act This law protects a very broad range of people with disabilities from discrimination. Petey would have been provided an equal opportunity to benefit from government services, including public education, transportation, and recreation. He would not have been confined to one room of the state hospital for most of his life.
Section 504 N/A Programs that receive government funding are prohibited from excluding individuals with disabilities. Instead of having doctors declare that Petey wouldn't respond to treatment, he would have been offered an equal opportunity to grown up and learn through public education and healthcare services.
Child Find N/A States must implement ways to identify, evaluate, and document all children with disabilities so that they receive education. Through Child Find, Petey would have been evaluated for a disability. This upholds a principle of zero reject, in which no children are prohibited from accessing an education that meets their support needs.

  1. Review all the IDEA disability categories. Under which disability category would Petey have been found eligible? Explain your reasons.

    Petey would be eligible for special education under the category Orthopedic Impairment. The IDEA includes cerebral palsy as an example of a cause for orthopedic impairments. He would also be eligible for speech-language services, as his facial deformities prevent Petey from being able to make sounds using his tongue. The IDEA definition for a speech or language impairment includes "stuttering, impaired articulation, a language impairment, or a voice impairment, that adversely affects a child's educational performance."

  2. Review the continuum of placements covered in Module 2. With current assistive technology and accommodations, which placement do you feel would be most appropriate for Petey and why?

    Petey is fully capable of receiving communication, and by using a wheelchair and assistive communication device, he should be educated mostly in a general education setting, following the principle of Least Restrictive Environment. He might spend some of the school day in a resource setting for speech and language services, such as helping him practice making sounds as well as using his assistive communication device. Additionally, activities in physical education would need to be adapted so Petey could participate, but he would likely be able to participate alongside nondisabled peers.

  3. Choose a particular age range (elementary, middle, high school). Identify 2 accommodations and 2 modifications that would have helped Petey be successful in school. Explain your reasons for choosing each.

    In middle school, Petey would use an assistive communication device to communicate concepts without needing to use his tongue. This device would likely be a touchscreen tablet with alternative and augmentative communication (AAC) capability that sorts words into categories and pairs them with icons, and can read the words aloud.
    Petey would also benefit from extended time on tests and assignments, because his limited fine motor control would make it difficult to accomplish reading and writing tasks.
    These tests and assignments might be modified into a closure format to reduce the effort that Petey must expend to complete them as well. The closure format involves replacing questions that expect a written response in complete sentences with a fill-in-the-blank approach.
    Finally, any oral presentations that Petey would have to do during middle school would be modified so that Petey could complete them in an alternative manner.

  4. Society has evolved in the inclusion of, and services for, people with disabilities. What else might the government and citizens do to help people with disabilities feel seen and welcome throughout all aspects of our society? (Your answer should be at least 4 sentences)

    Perhaps the inclusive practice that would benefit disabled people the most would be making healthcare much more accessible. Healthcare in the United States is prohibitively expensive to a majority of the population. Most of this medical care is provided by the private sector, and so access is determined almost exclusively by the fluctuations of the markets in the private sector. Although Petey found his own family throughout his life and touched countless lives through his story, he was separated from his parents before he could even remember them because they couldn't afford his medical care. He also spent a large portion of his life in a wheelchair that was modified for him by Owen Marsh, one of his caretakers, at his own expense. This was later replaced by a new wheelchair fundraised (and heavily discounted) for him by Trevor Ladd. Petey may have been able to access a lot more benefits, services, and overall enjoyment of life had he been able to access mobility aids and assistive technology devices more easily.
