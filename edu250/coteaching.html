Co-Teaching Observations

Collaboration and Co-Teaching

In this video, two teachers team together to teach a fifth-grade class about riddles. The majority of this lesson was team taught, and both instructors shared equally in the instruction. The teachers took turns speaking to the class as well as giving and answering questions. When the students started independently working on their riddles, the teachers began something more similar to one teach, one assist / observe; this gave both teachers the opportunity to provide any additional support needed by students in their classroom.

The team wanted to make the entire class feel included in the lesson by allowing the students to connect parts of themselves to what they are learning. This definitely came through in the lesson design, because every student was tasked with creating a riddle based on themself specifically. They took this a step further by helping specific students come up with ideas and write them into their four-square organizers. At the end of the lesson, they shared aloud some of the riddles, allowing some students to shine.

In their communication, the co-teachers always reacted positively to the other. They used their body language to convey that they were listening attentively. Finally, they made sure to hand off the role of the speaker to the other regularly, which increased the sense of parity they had in the classroom.

Being able to provide more individual help to a student who needed additional support worked very well, both for the teacher and the student. However, a drawback of this approach might have been that other students didn't get help with their questions, or that the classroom became noisy or unruly because of less classroom management.
