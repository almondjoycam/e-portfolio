About Camber
it / they

Throughout my own varied experiences with education, I notice patterns. My favourite teachers have been open-minded and encouraging; my least favourite teachers have inaccessible courses with content they don't seem to have a personal connection to.

I'm committed to accessibility because every single individual deserves to thrive. I'm passionate about technology because it can assist in turning this dream into reality, and because it can provide so much insight about ourselves.

I believe that the single greatest feeling in the universe occurs when a connection is made — be it physical, interpersonal, or theoretical. I both teach and learn because I keep seeing glimpses of this feeling of pure joy.

Who I Am

Every person is a diverse individual of infinite dimensions. I am not complete without any of my parts. For as long as I can remember, I've been creating visual art, and the creativity I've nurtured through art influences everything I do. Since then, I've picked up all manner of additional fixations, from the psychology of substances to the fictional linguistics encountered in video games. I want to both share my own passions
and be moved by the passions of others.

Though I can't remember it, I'm told that I taught myself to read. I vaguely remember asking my parents to teach me algebra, because the concept of methodically stripping away layers of mystery to reveal one final answer had such marvellous appeal. Even in creating this portfolio, I find myself searching for and learning new concepts about web design and development. I never want to stop learning.

I am a firm believer that both myself and my environment must exist in a state of wellness before any learning can occur. In my own experience, positive self-esteem is fundamental to well-being. However, this attribute is especially rare, because few people come with it; I am constantly reminding myself that sometimes, my internal monologue is unacceptable. I aim to uplift myself and everyone around me
in order to promote emotional well-being.

Why I Teach

Instructor at Code Ninjas, Burke, VA
March 2022 – present Perhaps what excites me the most about education is its bi-directionality. Within the past year, I have learned more about using the Unity game engine than I could possibly fathom learning on my own, and all solely because I have been teaching it. There are currently 15 children learning Unity at my location, and they've taught me more than they will ever know.

When our center is closed, I also hold virtual one-on-one sessions in Unity for several children — some I know from working with them in-person, and others several states away. It has been incredible watching them develop a passion for the games they create, but perhaps more importantly, we have spent this time building a sense of trust. Often, before we start working on the main game, I get to see their side projects on other platforms and hear about the events of their day.

With the current global climate of borders, tension, and hatred, the generation of youth is one of the few realms that remain unspoiled. I hope that the lesson I teach that stays for the longest is the lesson to keep an open mind and treat one another with kindness.
