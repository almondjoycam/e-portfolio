Hero of Change

Susan Burton

Susan Burton advocates for the rights of formerly incarcerated people, especially Black women.

After experiencing abuse and instability during childhood and the loss of her 5-year-old son, Burton developed a crack cocaine addiction which imprisoned her 6 times on drug-related charges.

Burton found treatment through the CLARE Foundation in Santa Monica, and has been clean ever since. She noticed that Santa Monica, a predominantly White area, had far more access to recovery resources than south Los Angeles, a predominately Black area, and felt compelled to bridge this gap in her home community.

Burton is the founder of A New Way of Life Re-entry Project, supporting formerly incarcerated women with housing, career services, and pro bono legal support.

This foundation is based on Burton's own experience with entering society after incarceration.

Burton believes that the prison system is unfairly punitive, and that public resources can be better invested in transformative services.

A New Way of Life also conducts voter outreach in Los Angeles area communities and jails, ensuring that all voters are registered and prepared to vote.
